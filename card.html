Egyptian Jerboa

These bipedal, keen hearing, jumping rodents can hop up to 25 kilometers an hour to escape predators.

  • Scientific Name: Jaculus orientalis
  • Average Body Length: 13cm
  • Average Tail Length: 20cm
  • Conservation Status: Least Concern

During the rainy season Jerboas create tunnels in mounds or hills to reduce the risk of flooding. In summer, occupying holes plug the entrance to keep out hot air and predators. Burrows are constructed with an emergency exit that ends just below the surface or at the surface but is not strongly obstructed, allowing the jerboa to quickly escape from predators.
